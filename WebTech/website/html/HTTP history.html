History of HTTP

Hypertext Transfer Protocol is a protocol in exchanging or transferring hypertext on the World Wide Web.A protocol is sent over TCP, or over a TLS-encrypted TCP connection,though any reliable transport protocol.HTTP is widely used to access resources (hypertext/multimedia on the World Wide Web and for fetching of resources, such as HTML documents.It is the foundation of data communication for the World Wide Web.The development was initiated by Tim Berners Lee at CERN in Conseill European on 1989 and jointly adopted by the W3C (World Wide Web Consortium) and the standard development of it was coordinated by theIETF (Internet Engineering Task Force).
